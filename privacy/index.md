# Privacy Policy for Audalume

**Effective date:** May 9, 2026  
**App name:** Audalume  
**Publisher:** Michael Clegg  
**Contact:** mcj@sdf.org

Audalume is an offline audiobook player for Windows. Audalume is designed to work entirely on your device. It does not require an account, does not connect to an online service, and does not include cloud sync, advertising, analytics, telemetry, or online tracking.

This Privacy Policy explains what information Audalume may access or store locally when you use the app.

## Information Audalume accesses

Audalume may access local files and folders that you choose to add to your audiobook library. This may include:

- audiobook files and folders selected by you;
- file names, folder paths, and file metadata;
- audiobook cover images or embedded artwork;
- audiobook duration, chapter information, and playback-related metadata;
- bookmarks, listening position, listening progress, and library organization data;
- app settings and preferences;
- local cache files or local diagnostic log files, if generated by the app.

Audalume does not access your files unless you select or add them through the app or otherwise make them available to the app through Windows.

## Information Audalume collects or transmits

Audalume does **not** collect, upload, transmit, sell, rent, share, or disclose your audiobook library data or listening activity.

Audalume does not use:

- user accounts;
- cloud storage;
- online sync;
- advertising networks;
- analytics services;
- telemetry services;
- tracking cookies;
- remote databases;
- third-party online APIs.

All audiobook library data handled by Audalume remains on your device.

## How Audalume uses local information

Audalume uses local information only to provide app features, including:

- displaying your audiobook library;
- remembering playback progress;
- saving bookmarks;
- showing cover art and metadata;
- organizing audiobook files;
- maintaining app settings;
- improving local app behavior and reliability.

This information is used locally by the app and is not sent to the publisher or to any third party by Audalume.

## Where information is stored

Audalume stores its app data locally on your Windows device, typically in the user's local application data area, such as:

```text
%LOCALAPPDATA%\Audalume
```

Depending on the installation method and Windows app packaging behavior, some app data may also be stored in the app's Windows package local data location.

## Data sharing and disclosure

Audalume does not share your personal information, audiobook files, library data, listening history, bookmarks, or settings with the publisher or with third parties.

Audalume does not sell personal information.

Audalume does not disclose personal information for advertising or marketing purposes.

## User controls

You control what audiobook folders and files are added to Audalume.

You can remove books, folders, bookmarks, or library entries from within the app where those options are available. You can also delete Audalume's local app data from your device.

To remove Audalume data, you may:

- remove library folders or books from the app;
- uninstall Audalume;
- reset the app through Windows settings, if available;
- delete Audalume's local app data folder from your Windows user profile.

Deleting local app data may remove saved library information, bookmarks, playback progress, settings, cache files, and local logs.

## Data security

Audalume stores data locally on your device. Access to that data is protected by your Windows user account, device permissions, and the security settings of your computer.

Audalume does not provide its own cloud backup or online account protection because it does not upload your data. You are responsible for protecting your Windows account, device, and local files.

## Children's privacy

Audalume does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Audalume does not include accounts, online communication, advertising, analytics, or online tracking.

## Microsoft Store and Windows

This Privacy Policy applies only to Audalume. If you install Audalume through the Microsoft Store, Microsoft may process information related to the Store, downloads, purchases, licensing, crash reporting, or Windows services under Microsoft's own privacy terms. Audalume does not control Microsoft's separate data practices.

## Changes to this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Any updates will be posted on this page with a new effective date.
